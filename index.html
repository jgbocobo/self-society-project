Introduction

Self-construction isn't a straight line. It is a winding path marked by challenges, lessons, and continuous discovery. As I reflect on my identity, I realize that who I am today comes from countless experiences, relationships, and moments of introspection.

02

My Journey Through Self

Perspectives of Self

I define myself as someone who has grown through many ups and downs. I have faced challenges and past traumas, but I stood up and got back on track every time. I lean more towards individualism. I value my own beliefs and prefer to forge my own path rather than just following the crowd.

Biological Influences

My genetics play a big role in who I am. The most important trait I inherited is resilience. My physical health is deeply connected to my self-worth. I believe that being healthy is a form of wealth, so prioritizing my health is always the best way forward.

Body Image

My relationship with my appearance is complex. I value how I present myself as a form of self-expression. I used to be a dancer, which gave me confidence. When I stopped after the pandemic, my perspective shifted. Now, I see body image as a reflection of my overall health and vitality.

Personality

I am a mix of introvert and extrovert. You could call me a "social chameleon" because I adapt depending on who I am with. I might seem reserved or intimidating at first, but people who get close to me discover that I am actually friendly, talkative, and caring.

Identity Development

Challenges have been the catalysts for my growth. Critical moments forced me to look inward. I play many roles in life, like being a student, a friend, and a brother. I am still exploring who I am, and I actively do things that scare me to push beyond my comfort zone.

Culture & Ethnicity

As a Filipino, respect is the cultural practice that matters most to me. It shows politeness, dignity, and community spirit. I try to balance honoring my roots with being progressive. It is a constant negotiation between tradition and modern values.

Sexuality

My understanding of sexuality has evolved. I moved from a binary view to a deep appreciation of the human spectrum. This learning made me more open-minded and empathetic. It enriched my perspective on identity and how we connect with one another.

Mental Wellbeing

I rely on hobbies like billiards to manage stress and clear my mind. Taking intentional pauses is crucial for me. I am currently happy with where I am heading. I am taking slow but steady steps towards better wellbeing.

Through Others' Eyes

"A chameleon who fits his behavior based on the dynamics of the group."

I interviewed three close friends—Eloisa, Catrina, and Christian—to see how they perceive me as a friend and a member of society.

They described me as boastful yet capable, and confident yet humble. They see me as a "chameleon" because I adapt to get along with others. While they initially found me intimidating, they now see me as a caring, "sibling-like" figure who checks on them.


Personal Reflection:
This feedback was somewhat expected, but I was surprised by how much they look up to me. Realizing that I impact their habits and perspectives is humbling. It motivates me to be more intentional about the influence I have on the people I care about.

My Manifestos

Personal Manifesto

Who I Am & Who I'm Becoming

My Life Purpose

"I am here to be myself and show the world that being me means being truthful and honest. Authenticity is the greatest gift we can offer to ourselves and others."

What I Believe

By leaving your comfort zone, you'll be able to do most things you believed you couldn't. Discomfort is where growth happens, and embracing challenges is how we discover our true potential.

My Core Values

These aren't just words I picked because they sound good. These are the things that actually matter to me, tested by real situations and the people I've met along the way.

Loyalty

This is number one for me, no question. You know why? Because I've seen what happens when people aren't loyal. I've had friends who were there when everything was fun and easy, but disappeared the moment things got tough. And I've had friends who stuck around through my worst days—when I was dealing with that brutal breakup, when I wasn't the most pleasant person to be around.

Loyalty to me means showing up. It means if you say you're my friend, you actually mean it. When my friends Eloisa, Catrina, and Christian said I check on them when they're down, that's loyalty in action. I do it because that's what real friendship looks like. Not just hanging out when it's convenient, but actually caring about what happens to the people in your circle.

I'm the type who won't offer friendship easily, but once I consider you a friend, I'm all in. I'll maintain that relationship, I'll show up, I'll remember the small things. And I expect the same back. If you're loyal to me, I'll be loyal to you—it's that simple. But break that trust? That's something I won't forget.

Honesty

I can't stand fake people. Like, I really can't. In a world where everyone's trying to show their best version on social media, where people say one thing and do another, being honest is almost rare. But it's so important to me.

I'd rather you tell me the hard truth than a comfortable lie. When my friends called me "boastful," I actually appreciated that they were honest about it. They added that I have the capabilities to back it up, which was nice, but the fact that they felt comfortable enough to say something that might hurt my feelings? That's real honesty.

I try to live this way too. If I think something, I'll say it—not in a mean way, but in a real way. People might find me intimidating at first because of this. I don't sugarcoat things. But those who get past that initial impression realize I'm just being genuine. I'd rather be real and risk someone not liking me than pretend to be someone I'm not.

Empathy

I'll be honest—empathy doesn't come naturally to me. I'm working on it. I used to be the type who'd think "just get over it" when people complained about their problems. But life has a way of teaching you lessons, doesn't it?

When I went through my own struggles—that breakup that knocked me down, the times I felt like I wasn't good enough—I realized how hard life actually is. How much we all need someone to understand without judging. Now I get it. Now when my friends are going through something, I try to actually feel what they're feeling, not just hear what they're saying.

I read psychology books now, trying to understand how people think and why they act certain ways. Not to manipulate anyone, but to connect better. To be the kind of friend who actually gets it. I'm still learning, still growing in this area. But that's the point—I'm trying. And I need people around me who understand that growth isn't instant, who can be patient with me as I figure this out.

Compassion

This is different from empathy—at least to me. Empathy is understanding what someone feels. Compassion is doing something about it. And man, do I respect people who show real compassion.

My parents are like this. They've overcome so many problems in their lives, and instead of becoming bitter, they became kinder. That resilience I inherited from them? It came with a side of compassion. They taught me that being strong doesn't mean being hard.

When my friends described me as someone who checks on them, as someone "sibling-like" who cares—that meant everything to me. Because that's the kind of person I want to be. Not just someone who understands your pain, but someone who actually helps carry it. Whether it's inviting you to play billiards to take your mind off things, or just sitting with you when you need company.

I want to influence people to be more compassionate too. The world's rough enough as it is. We need more people who give a damn about each other.

Growth

I'm obsessed with growth, but not in that toxic productivity way. I'm not trying to be perfect or hustle 24/7. I'm talking about real growth—the kind that actually changes you as a person.

You know what's funny? I used to think I knew everything. I used to dismiss sexuality as "there's only boys and girls, what's the big deal?" Now I understand that I was ignorant. That learning changed me. That's growth—being willing to admit you were wrong and actually changing your perspective.

I actively do things that scare me. Not stupid things, but things outside my comfort zone. Why? Because that's where growth happens. That breakup that devastated me? It forced me to grow. It made me realize there were so many things I needed to improve about myself. And I did the work.

Growth to me means becoming a better version of myself without losing who I am at the core. It means being open to new experiences while staying true to my values. It's a slow process—I'm taking steady steps, not rushing. But I'm committed to it for life.

My Commitments

  • ✓ I will continue to influence people and persuade them to be compassionate. The world needs more kindness.
  • ✓ I will be loyal to those people who are loyal to me. Loyalty is reciprocal.
  • ✓ I will prioritize the quality of relationships rather than the quantity. Deep, meaningful connections matter more.

My Non-Negotiables

  • ✗ I will never cover the truth with a lie. Truth is sacred, and I refuse to compromise it.
  • ✗ I will never be apologetic for being who I am. I've worked hard to understand and accept myself.
  • ✗ I will never break promises because it's convenient for me. My word is my bond.

Health Manifesto

A Holistic Commitment to Wellness

I commit to caring for myself holistically, recognizing that true health encompasses all dimensions of my being. Health is not a destination but a daily practice, and I commit to showing up for myself.

Physical Health

I try to have hobbies that include sports—killing two birds with one stone by enjoying myself while staying healthy. Physical activity isn't just about fitness; it's about joy and vitality. I commit to finding activities that nourish both my body and spirit.

Mental Health

I try to read books about managing mental health and study the psychology of humans to better understand myself and others. I practice not being absorbed by other people's problems, maintaining healthy boundaries. I commit to intellectual growth and emotional boundaries.

Emotional Health

Every once in a while, I sit with myself for a good thirty minutes to reflect on the decisions I've chosen. If I distinguish that a critical decision kept me safe from mental harm—even though it might not be the most desirable option—I will choose it for my own sanity.

Social Health

I prioritize quality time with my friends almost every other day by inviting them to my hobbies, like playing billiards. We occasionally drink to celebrate our small wins. Relationships are central to wellbeing, and I commit to nurturing the connections that matter most.

Spiritual Health

I try to walk around UPLB once in a while to enjoy the sceneries, trees, and the goodness of slow life. I try to talk to God once in a while to be guided. Spiritual health isn't about rigid practice—it's about finding moments of peace, connection, and meaning.

07

Conclusion

The self is not a fixed destination but a continuous unfolding. I am here for the entire journey.

What stands out most is that I am still in the process of becoming. I don't have all the answers, and I am comfortable with that uncertainty. Through the eyes of others, I've seen that my impact extends beyond what I realize. My manifestos serve as guideposts—living documents that will evolve as I do.
